About Me – Chinedu Ogbonna

I believe media storytelling captures moments in ways words alone cannot. So allow me to tell my story — one medium at a time.

Rock Bottom: The Beginning of My Rebuild

The picture above captures the lowest point in my life.

I had just dropped out of engineering school. I lost my job. I lost my apartment. And on the eve of my Google Africa Developer Scholarship exam — the one I had prepared for months — I was robbed. My laptop and phone? Gone.

At that moment, I had nothing:

  • No job.
  • No phone.
  • No accomdation of my own
  • Nothing to my name

But rock bottom has a strange power — it forces you to choose: stay down or rise up. And from that moment… Stiil I rise.

Still I rise

I’m not trying to sound like a motivational speaker—really, I’m not. And this isn’t one of those “I beat the odds” kind of stories either. But the truth is, despite the setbacks, I chose to keep going.

After everything, I locked in and taught myself digital marketing. I helped companies grow their portfolios and scaled user acquisition. Then I made a bold move—into product marketing. Guess what? I thrived. I’m still thriving. My income surpassed my expectations, and for the first time in a long time, I was genuinely loving the work.

Then, on a random Sunday, I got an email. DevCareer X Bybit. A scholarship. Shared by Hck Sultan. And just like that, the old spark returned. The curiosity. The desire. I watched a few videos. I felt it again. And I applied—this time, to pivot into Cloud.

Will it be tough balancing this with two jobs? Absolutely. But what is life without learning?
